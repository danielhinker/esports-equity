Frequently Asked Questions

eSports Equity is a brand new funding portal formed under the JOBS act of 2012. We operate under title 3 of the law known as reg CF, which allows investors from across the entirety of the financial spectrum. This means investors with $10,000 in assets have access to the same investment opportunities as investors with $10,000,000 in assets.

eSports Equity acts as a host for teams and companies in the eSports scene who are looking for money to grow their brand. Investors could range from outside investors looking to enter the eSports market to people who have been passionate fans of the team for years.

Just log in or register a new account and click the 'start a project' button. After you fill in some preliminary information, we will reach out to you to complete the process.

It depends on your needs. We suggest that if you need to raise a lot of money then leave enough time for enough people to see your offerings over a few months. If you only need a small capital injection, then you can have a shorter campaign of a few weeks.

The regulations that eSports Equity abide by require that certain investor safety measures be taken. This means that investors have the option to pull money out of your team

We charge
- 5% of a successfully raised campaign
- $1,600 to create an escrow account after a successful raise
- Any additional costs related to that process taken from the total raised

We don't charge investors with any fees.

You create an account on our website and access the page of the team/company you want to invest in. You then follow the guidelines provided from that point onward to invest into the team.

These are based on limitations set by the company raising money. These limits are applied to investors so if for example, a company sets a $50.00 limit on the team then someone looking to invest any amount lower than $50.00 would be unable to do so. Likewise with a maximum limit of $50.00, anyone looking to invest more than $50.00 would be unable to do so.

We are operating under the laws and regulations of the United States of America, there may be other countries with laws similar that allow investment but we cannot make any recommendations for any investors outside of the U.S. who might want to utilize our service. If you fall under this category, we strongly suggest you update yourself on the laws in your country to see if you can legally invest.

Unfortunately we are not accepting teams or companies not based in the U.S.
